Simple Present Tense

Definition

The simple present tense is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements. It's one of the most commonly used tenses in English.

Functions

  • To express habits, general truths, repeated actions or unchanging situations
  • To give instructions or directions
  • To express fixed arrangements, present or future
  • To tell stories or narrate events (for dramatic effect)

Examples

1. I work in a bank. (habit)

2. The sun rises in the east. (general truth)

3. Water boils at 100Â°C. (scientific fact)

4. Our train leaves at 9:00 AM tomorrow. (fixed arrangement)

Formula

Positive: Subject + V1 (s/es for third person singular) + object

Negative: Subject + do/does + not + V1 + object

Question: Do/Does + subject + V1 + object?

Examples:

- She works at a hospital.

- They don't like coffee.

- Does he speak French?

Simple Past Tense

Definition

The simple past tense is used to talk about completed actions or events that happened at a specific time in the past. It describes actions that are completely finished.

Functions

  • To express an action that started and finished at a specific time in the past
  • To describe a series of completed actions in the past
  • To talk about habits in the past
  • To describe past facts or generalizations
  • To talk about past states or situations

Examples

1. I went to school yesterday. (completed action)

2. She didn't like coffee when she was young. (past habit)

3. People paid much less for houses 50 years ago. (past fact)

4. He lived in Paris for five years. (past state)

Formula

Positive: Subject + V2 + object

Negative: Subject + did + not + V1 + object

Question: Did + subject + V1 + object?

Examples:

- We visited the museum last week.

- She didn't finish her homework.

- Did you see that movie?

Simple Future Tense

Definition

The simple future tense is used to talk about actions or events that will happen in the future. It can express predictions, promises, plans, or willingness.

Functions

  • To predict a future event (often with think, believe, expect, etc.)
  • To express willingness (voluntary actions)
  • To make a promise or offer
  • To express a plan or intention
  • To make a spontaneous decision

Examples

1. It will rain tomorrow. (prediction)

2. I will help you with your homework. (willingness)

3. We will meet at 5 pm. (plan)

4. I think I will have the steak. (spontaneous decision)

Formula

Positive: Subject + will/shall + V1 + object

Negative: Subject + will/shall + not + V1 + object

Question: Will/Shall + subject + V1 + object?

Examples:

- They will arrive at 8 PM.

- I won't forget your birthday. (will not)

- Shall we go for a walk? (suggestion)
